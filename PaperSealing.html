    Have Any Questions About Paper Sealing?

    Paper sealing is a process that ensures documents are securely closed or sealed to protect their contents. This process is typically used for confidential, sensitive, or important papers and is commonly found in legal, governmental, and corporate settings.

    The main benefits of paper sealing include enhanced security, ensuring that the contents of a document remain protected from unauthorized access or tampering. It also helps maintain the integrity of sensitive information and provides proof of confidentiality or non-disclosure when required.

    Materials used for paper sealing include special waxes, adhesive seals, embossed stamps, and security labels. These materials are designed to create an irreversible seal that cannot be opened without showing visible signs of tampering.

    A tampered paper seal will often show signs of breakage, smearing, or discoloration. The adhesive or wax may also appear disturbed, and in some cases, a broken embossed seal can indicate that the document has been altered. If you suspect tampering, you should verify the integrity of the seal immediately.
